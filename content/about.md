# About WatchPost Advocacy

<div class="aux-intro">
WatchPost Advocacy is an independent survivor advocacy and resource platform for students, families, and survivors of school sexual abuse and Title IX violations in Missouri and Illinois. Published by <strong>Rights Watch Media Group LLC</strong>.
</div>

## Our Mission

School sexual abuse is a systemic problem with consistent patterns across Missouri and Illinois: an adult in a position of authority, a student who trusted them, and an institution that managed its legal exposure while ignoring the student's harm. Title IX has existed since 1972. Mandatory reporting laws have existed for decades. The failures continue because institutions are not held accountable and survivors often do not understand their rights until their legal deadlines have passed.

WatchPost Advocacy exists to change that. We publish plain-language guides to Title IX law, school abuse accountability, survivor legal rights, and the documentary record of institutional failures — organized around the specific districts, coaches, administrators, and policies that shaped outcomes in Missouri and Illinois schools.

## What We Cover

- **Title IX rights and the OCR complaint process** — what filing a complaint means, what it costs, what it achieves
- **Civil lawsuit rights for school abuse survivors** — Missouri and Illinois statutes of limitations, deliberate indifference standard, notice requirements
- **Institutional accountability records** — OCR findings and resolution agreements, state educator certification disciplinary actions, MSHSAA coaching records
- **Mandatory reporting law** — Missouri and Illinois reporting obligations, what happens when they are violated, and what recourse survivors have
- **School board accountability** — public records showing what administrators knew and when they knew it
